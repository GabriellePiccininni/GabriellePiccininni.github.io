ABOUT

I recently graduated from a Front-End Web Development course at General Assembly where I learned HTML, CSS, & jQuery and how to be an awesome Front-End Web Developer! During my spare time, I enjoy painting, writing, reading, cooking, and soaking up the sun.

Growing up with many siblings who always wanted to play with the same toy or have the same dessert, we were always in need of a cretive solution (or a way to convince the others that they really wanted the Creamcicle instead of the Choco Taco). I've always loved problem solving and it often informed my approaches to art projects and research papers when I studied Studio Art and Literature in college.
In graduate school, I studied publishing technology and theorized and balanced profit & loss statements to creatively combat the obstacles of the constantly changing publishing industry. I currently work as an eLearning editor developing content, GIS maps, and video that exist within interactive eBooks and companion websites for higher education textbooks. I create websites within a Drupal template but am limited to the front-end UI. The GA FEWD course stengthened my skill set and was the spark that started my shift into a new career of web development. I want to get back creative strategizing and problem solving. But instead of Chaco Tacos, I want to work with clients to tackle requests, guide visions, and develop products and technology for this ever-changing landscape.
